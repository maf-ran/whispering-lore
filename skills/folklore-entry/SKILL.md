# Folklore Entry Skill

## Purpose
Define the complete structure and rules for generating encyclopedia entries for creatures, stories, and legends.

---

## Article Structure

### 1. Header
- **Title**
- **Aliases**
- **Alternative Names**
- **Historical Spellings**

### 2. Classification
- **Type**
- **Country**
- **Region**
- **Subregion / Village (if known)**
- **Motif Numbers (ATU, ML, etc.)**
- **Cultural Context**

### 3. Description
- Clear, neutral summary
- Physical appearance
- Behavior
- Abilities or traits
- Weaknesses (if applicable)

### 4. Habitat
- Natural or supernatural environment
- Regional variations

### 5. Behavior
- Typical actions
- Interactions with humans
- Patterns or cycles

### 6. Cultural Significance
- Symbolism
- Social role
- Rituals
- Historical importance

### 7. Variants
- Regional differences
- Narrative differences
- Appearance differences
- Motif differences
- Provide sources for each variant

### 8. Historical Notes
- First recorded appearance
- Collector or researcher
- Archive or publication
- Evolution over time

### 9. Related Entities
- Similar creatures
- Related stories
- Motif-linked entities

### 10. Sources
- Only include sources listed in `data/sources.md`
- Add new sources to `sources.md` before generating entry

---

## Output Format
- Markdown
- Follow structure strictly
