# Top Folklore Experts & Scholars Worldwide

## Legendary Founders
- **Jacob Grimm** & **Wilhelm Grimm** - German - Founded folklore studies with Grimm's Fairy Tales (1812)
- **Franz Boas** - American - Father of American anthropology, cultural relativism
- **Alan Dundes** - American - Leading folklorist, psychoanalytic approach, "Cinderella: A Casebook"
- **Vladimir Propp** - Russian - Structural analysis of folktales, "Morphology of the Folktale"
- **Bronislaw Malinowski** - Polish-British - Functionalist approach to folklore

## European Scholars
- **Maria Tatar** - Harvard - Fairy tales, Grimm, Germanic folklore
- **Jack Zipes** - Minnesota - Fairy tales as cultural power, 60+ books
- **John Lindow** - UC Berkeley - Norse mythology, Scandinavian folklore
- **Gregory Nagy** - Harvard - Greek mythology, classical studies
- **Patricia Lysaght** - Ireland - Irish supernatural folklore
- **Linda Dégh** - Indiana University - Legend and belief studies
- **Henry Glassie** - Indiana - Material culture, folk arts
- **Simon Bronner** - Penn State - American folklore, folklife
- **Ülo Valk** - University of Tartu - Estonian and comparative folklore
- **Stephen Mitchell** - Harvard - Scandinavian folklore, Nordic traditions
- **Jeana Jorgensen** - Folklore 101, fairy tales

## Asian Scholars
### Japanese
- **Michael Dylan Foster** - UC Davis - Leading yokai scholar, "Book of Yōkai", "Pandemonium and Parade"
- **Komatsu Kazuhiko** - International Research Center (Nichibunken) - Premier Japanese yokai expert
- **Yumoto Kōichi** - Kawasaki City Museum - 5,000+ yokai collection
- **Zack Davisson** - NYU - Yūrei (ghosts), yokai translator
- **Noriko T. Reider** - Miami University - Japanese demons, oni
- **Yasui Manami** - Nichibunken - Yokai database, body folklore
- **Yanagita Kunio** (deceased) - Father of Japanese folkloristics
